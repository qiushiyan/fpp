8.2 Methods with trend

\[ \begin{aligned} \text{Forecast equation} \;\;\; \hat{y}_{t+h|t} &= \ell_t + hb_t \\ \text{Level equation} \;\;\;\;\;\;\;\; \ell_t &= \alpha y_t + (1 - \alpha) \ell_{t-1} \\ \text{Trend equation} \;\;\;\;\;\;\; b_t &= \beta^*(\ell_t - \ell_{t - 1}) + (1 - \beta^*)b_{t-1} \end{aligned} \]

\(b_t\) denotes the estimated slope of the series at time \(t\). For \(b_t\) is an essentially weighted average of slope at \(t=1, t = 2, \cdots, t = t - 1\). The following equation shows thta \(\beta_0^*(\ell_t - \ell_{t - 1})\) is a weight \(\beta_0^*\) attatched to the estimated slope at time \(t\)

\[ \begin{split} \ell_t - \ell_{t - 1} &= [(\hat{y}_{t+1|t} - b_t) - (\hat{y}_{t|t-1} - b_{t-1})] \\ &= \hat{y}_{t+1|t} - \hat{y}_{t|t-1} - (b_t - b_{t-1}) \\ &= \frac{(\hat{y}_{t+1|t} - \hat{y}_{t|t-1})}{1} + \frac{(b_t - b_{t-1})}{1} \end{split} \]
